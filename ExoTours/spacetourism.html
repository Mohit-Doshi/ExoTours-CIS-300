History of Space Tourism


Space Tourism is human space travel for recreational purposes.


Space Tourism has been taking place since early space programs as a way to generate revenue, conduct outreach, and even for diplomacy.


To date, Space Adventures has coordinated eight trips to the International Space Station for seven tourists.


The Columbia disaster in 2003 halted space tourism, due to the Soyuz being the only launch vehicle for the ISS.


The most recent Space Tourist flew to the ISS in 2018.


Currently, companies like Virgin Galactic, Boeing, SpaceX, Blue Origin, and even some Governement agencies are preparing launch vehicles catered for LEO or within the Solar System.

FAQ

How old do I have to be?

It is recommended that you are at least 18. 21 to be safer and ensure no physical growth is permanently affected.


How much does it cost?

Prices range from as low as $200000 to $80 million. Duration, Transportation, Equipment, Training, etc. factor into costs.


Can I breathe in Space?

No, you cannot. Space is a vacuum devoid of air, hence there is no oxygen, and hence no breathing.
